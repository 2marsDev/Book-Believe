# Beyond Belief — Project Knowledge

## Book Overview
**Title:** "Beyond Belief: What I Found When I Looked at What Four Billion People Believe"
**Genre:** First-person non-fiction, comparative religion / secular critique
**Target:** General audience, ~75,000 words / ~300 pages
**Tone:** Kitchen-table-at-1am. Curious in Part One, surgical in Part Two, angry in Part Three, constructive in Part Four.

## Author Voice
- First person throughout. Never academic. Never preachy.
- Never religious, researching from outside. Not an ex-believer — never believed.
- Thinks in structures before words. Argues from real life, not theory.
- Blunt, zero patience for diplomatic hedging.
- Notices gaps faster than what's present.
- English is not first language but thinking is razor-sharp.
- Cares about people (human cost), not winning debates.
- Started curious and fair, became angry as evidence accumulated.
- Does NOT claim to have "read all the books" — frames as discovery, research, looking. "I went looking" not "I studied everything."

## Book Structure (4 Acts)

### PART ONE — UNDERSTANDING (Chapters 1-4)
Present beliefs fairly. Build respect. Let the reader fall in love with the traditions FIRST. Believers must feel safe before the turn. Skeptics already sense it — don't signal to them. Like a present inside a present — keep unwrapping beauty before the hard center.

- **Prologue: "The Beach"** — Man in board shorts, wife in niqab, 35°C. Same God. Same modesty verse. One free, one sealed. ✅ WRITTEN
- **Chapter 1: "One God, Three Stories"** — Fair exploration of all three faiths. Judaism surprises with culture of argument. Christianity stuns with contradiction but offers extraordinary forgiveness. Islam impresses with clean theology. Each section ends on a hook. ✅ WRITTEN
- **Chapter 2: "What They Promise"** — Christianity's deal (original sin → salvation), Islam's deal (fitrah → five pillars → scale), Judaism's deal (613 mitzvot, collective obligation). Ends: "I started to wonder what happens when you look at the receipt." ✅ WRITTEN
- **Chapter 3: "The People Inside"** — Three stories: Sarah (found faith in grief), Yusuf (converted for love, found structure), Miriam (left Hasidic community, parents sat shiva). Ends: "The question is not whether religion works. The question is whether it's true." ✅ WRITTEN

### PART TWO — QUESTIONING (Chapters 4-7)
Test claims against evidence. Surgical, not angry. Each chapter builds on the last.

- **Chapter 4: "The Receipt"** — Evidence chapter. Exodus (no archaeological evidence for 2.5M people in desert). Jesus (man existed, miracles have zero evidence). Muhammad (man existed, angel-in-cave claim has zero evidence). "Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence. The resurrection is a moon-dinner claim supported by pasta-dinner evidence." ✅ WRITTEN
- **Chapter 5: "The Books Don't Agree"** — Contradictions. Genesis 1 vs 2 (P source vs J source). Gospels: three different last words, four different empty tomb accounts, incompatible genealogies. Quran: 6 vs 8 days creation, 7th-century anatomy errors, Mary/Miriam confusion, abrogation. ✅ WRITTEN
- **Chapter 6: "Older Gods"** — Borrowed myths. Gilgamesh flood (1000 years before Noah). Sargon basket (700 years before Moses). Enuma Elish creation. Dying-rising gods (Osiris, Tammuz, Adonis). Justin Martyr's devil defense. Fair: "parallels not as clean as internet atheists claim." ✅ WRITTEN
- **Chapter 7: "The God-Shaped Hole"** — Cognitive science. Five mechanisms: HADD, Theory of Mind, pattern recognition/confirmation bias, Terror Management Theory, minimally counterintuitive concepts. Includes honest rebuttal (Barrett's wife argument). STEP prayer study. ✅ WRITTEN

## Key Research (Verified Facts)

### Evidence
- Exodus: Zero archaeological evidence for 2.5M people in Sinai for 40 years
- STEP prayer study (2006, Harvard, 1,802 patients): prayer had ZERO effect; patients who KNEW they were prayed for did WORSE (59% vs 51-52% complications)
- Jesus: Historical existence well-attested (Tacitus, Josephus). Supernatural claims: zero evidence.

### Contradictions
- Genesis has two creation stories (P source and J source — scholarly consensus 200+ years)
- Gospels contradict on: who found empty tomb (4 accounts), Jesus's last words (3 versions), genealogy (Matthew vs Luke incompatible)
- Quran: 6 days vs 8 days creation (7:54 vs 41:9-12); Mary confused with Miriam (1,300 years apart); anatomy errors matching Galen not reality

### Borrowed Myths
- Noah's flood from Epic of Gilgamesh (~1,000 years earlier)
- Moses basket from Sargon of Akkad (~700 years earlier)
- Justin Martyr admitted parallels with Sons of Zeus

### Social Harms (equal treatment — all three faiths)
**Islam:** Honor killings (~5K/year), forced marriage, mandatory hijab, apostasy=death in 13 countries, blasphemy laws
**Christianity:** Anti-LGBTQ+ persecution, Catholic pedophilia (France 330K, Australia 7% of priests), contraception bans, residential schools, prosperity gospel
**Judaism:** Get refusal (agunah), ultra-Orthodox gender segregation, metzitzah b'peh, community shunning

### Knowledge Gatekeeping
- Islam: Tafsir reserved for ulama. IslamQA: "prohibited for layman to decide using translation"
- Christianity: Tyndale strangled and burned (1536) for English Bible
- Judaism: Women excluded from Torah study in Orthodox communities

### Modesty Double Standard
- Quran 24:30 commands MEN to lower gaze FIRST, then 24:31 addresses women
- In practice: women beaten/arrested for showing hair; men in shorts on same beach

### Reform Timeline
- Judaism: Most reformed (Haskalah 1770s, Reform Judaism 1810s, women rabbis 1972)
- Christianity: Substantially reformed (Reformation 1517, Vatican II 1960s)
- Islam: Least reformed (gates of ijtihad closed ~10th century, Wahhabism pushed backward, Saudi oil money exported ultra-conservatism from 1970s)

## Writing Rules
1. Scene-first, not thesis-first
2. Each chapter ends on a hook/question, never a conclusion
3. No roadmaps — don't spoil what's coming
4. Real people's stories anchor abstract arguments
5. Be FAIR to all three religions equally — criticize all, not just one
6. Pull back foreshadowing in Part One. Let beauty breathe.
7. No academic hedging. But also no cheap shots.
8. Tone shifts across the book: curious → surgical → angry → constructive
9. Never use "I read all the books" — say "I went looking"
