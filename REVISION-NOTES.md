# Revision Notes

Tracked feedback to apply after first draft completion.

## Tone & Structure

1. **Part One needs to be warmer and longer.** Don't show skepticism too early. Let the reader fall in love with the traditions first. Believers need to feel safe before the turn. Skeptics will already sense it — they don't need signals. Like a present inside a present — keep unwrapping beauty before the hard center appears.

2. **Don't claim "I read all the books."** It's not authentic and sharp readers will question it. Frame as discovery, research, conversations — not comprehensive scholarship. "I went looking" works better than "I studied everything."

3. **Pull back foreshadowing in Part One.** Lines like "but then I looked at the evidence" tip the hand too early. Let the beauty breathe. The turn should surprise even when it shouldn't.

## Fact-Check Corrections (pending)

4. **Trinity formalization:** Nicaea (325 CE) established the creed, but the Trinity doctrine was formalized at Constantinople (381 CE). Fix in abrahamic-religions.html.

5. **December 25 origin:** Two competing scholarly theories — Sol Invictus substitution vs. calculation theory. Saturnalia was Dec 17-23, NOT Dec 25. Fix in abrahamic-religions.html.

6. **Lemaître:** Was a diocesan Catholic priest, not Augustinian (Mendel was Augustinian).
